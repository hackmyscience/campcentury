The official purpose of Camp Century was ice core drilling, and its data is still regularly cited in scientific journals on climatology today.
In 1950, The U.S. Army started a covert project to build a series of underground rocket-launch sites, under the ice of Greenland.
These sites were manned by up to 200 men at any one time, and powered by a nuclear reactor. The research about group dynamics and team building were revolutionary for the time.
